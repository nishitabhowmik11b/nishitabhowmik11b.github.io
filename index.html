COVID-19 COVID-19 is an infectious disease caused by a novel strain of coronavirus called Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus 2 (SARS-CoV-2) which began to spread in Wuhan district of China in late 2019. Today, it has become one of the largest pandemics that humanity has ever faced and is a cause for global concern.
THE CORONAVIRUS
The first step to victory is to know the enemy. Here is a brief history and analysis of the organism that shut down the world
What is it?
Coronaviruses are a group of RNA viruses that cause illness in birds and mammals. In human beings, many diseases are caused by their strains, from mild ones like common cold (also caused by various other viruses) to more severe ones like SARS, MERS and COVID-19. They are spherical particles with projections. It derives its name from the Latin word "corona" which means crown, which is also the name of the outer layer of the sun's atmosphere.
History
Scientists first identified a human coronavirus in 1965. Seven coronaviruses can infect humans. The one that causes SARS emerged in southern China in 2002 and quickly spread to 28 other countries. More than 8,000 people were infected by July 2003, and 774 died. MERS started in Saudi Arabia in 2012. It has the same respiratory symptoms but can also cause kidney failure. Experts say SARS-CoV-2 originated in bats.
Is there a cure?
Unfortunately, there is no definite cure or vaccine for it yet, but research is ongoing. Viral diseases are almost never treated by drugs that kill the virus, but drugs that help relieve the symptoms and support overall health, while the body's immune system fights the virus itself. This is because viruses enter our own cells and use them to multiply and spread. Any efforts to kill the virus would also negatively effect our cells and cause harm.

PRECAUTIONS The fate of our country is in our hands. We must be careful in this time of crisis. Here are some ways to keep you and people around you safe from the disease.
Hygeine
The virus can survive on surfaces for multiple hours, so it is important to keep all surfaces clean and wash our hands regularly. Hands must be washed for at least 20 seconds and sanitizer used should have at least 60% alcohol. Soap and alcohol-based sanitizers kill the virus by destroying its lipid envelope.
Wear a Mask
The virus is spread through droplet infection, meaning that the virus travels through droplets of saliva and mucus that are expelled when an infected person coughs, sneezes or even talks. Wearing a mask has been proven to nearly eliminate this spread entirely. While a mask is worn for the purpose of protecting others from yourself, it can also help you not touch your face, reducing the risk for you too.
Social Distancing
The expulsion of droplets through coughing and sneezing allows them to travel further than you might think, and even stay suspended in air for a period of time. To stay safe, everyone must maintain a minimum distance of 6 feet from each other. It is strongly suggested that you stay at home unless it is absolutely necessary to go outside, since that limits your exposure to potentially infected people.
LOCKDOWN WELLBEING While we all are stuck at home, it is essential that we take care of our overall health and wellbeing. Here are some suggestions on how to do so.
Mental Health
It is critical to assert the importance of mental health and stability in this time of chaos. With the shift in lifestyle came an abrupt decline in social interaction, an essential part of human nature, which impacted most of us negatively. It is crucial that we are able to preserve our psychological balance by engaging with family and friends while being miles away from them. Depression and anxiety rates have risen, and if you are suffering from either of those, seek help from people you trust or a professional. Mental health is just as important as physical health.
Physical Health
While the modern lifestyle is already quite sedentary, lockdown has caused us all to become more even more so. Moreover, with days and nights blending into each other to form a slurry of broken sleep cycles, people's health is at the risk of serious deterioration. One must not let themself go down a spiral of bad health, and to do that, a combination of good diet, exercise and daily routine is necessary. Eat nutritious food to keep your immunity up, sleep at least 7-9 hours a day and on time, and form a habit of exercising daily. Staying healthy will keep you safer during this pandemic, as well as improve your mood.
